
## The Forcefield

The Forcefield was first developed by the nomadic Samond people, who used it to protect their ships from pirates. 
However, many Samond themselves also became notorious for using the forcefield and its optical counterpart, cloaking, to 
raid planets without being detected. 

## Dark Matter Weaponry

Dark Matter Weaponry was first attested by Sir William Ttur as a technology used by the Ttur people. William thought of
the technology as extremely unethical, writing:

*"The Ttur today are already burdened by a most loathsome reputation of merciless,
bloodthirsty creatures. The use of Dark Matter Weaponry only serves to clarify this reputation as 
the truth, or something quite close to it."*

Ttur led the movement to ban the weapons throughout the galaxy, but their sheer effectiveness both as a deterrent and a 
tool of conquest has led to their continued use. 

## Faster Than Light

The term "faster than light" is a slight misnomer, as spacefaring civilisations have long bee able to travel at speeds 
exceeding that of light. The name of the technology comes from old texts which speak of "faster than light" as a threshold
to break to enable intergalactic travel.

Instead, this newfound technology has enabled spacefaring like never before, with the ability to travel without regard for the bounds of space.

## Hypercommerce

As the galactic population continued to rise, so too did its demand for minerals. The famed merchant and explorer Wilhelm Schniebler von Franken 
pioneered the technology of Hypercommerce as a way of transmitting minerals through ordinary communication channels. He compared
the new technology to the digital currencies once pioneered by pre-spacefaring civilisation, saying:

*"Long ago, sentient peoples already considered physical currency to be an obsolete medium. With Hypercommerce, we can consider even physical commodities the same."*

## The Singularity

General Aggripina Aquila of the eponymous Empire was the first to develop a real, functioning Singularity, which had long been 
theorised by many civilisations. Its initial development was supposed to have a militaristic application, but its true success was 
determined to be in citybuilding. The ability to create a pocket of space seemingly from nothing proved revolutionary in colonial development.

## Orbital Defence

The mysterious Dirk Order long believed in the coming of an Apocalypse, in which a mysterious spacefaring entity would 
come to destroy them. In response, Grandmaster Bulgaris ordered the development of a revolutionary defensive infrastructure. 
An enormous cannon orbiting the Dirk homeworld was created that would serve as a first line of defence was created.

The Dirk's renowned paranoia came into use when an army of Ttur warriors invaded. Even the fearsome Ttur were no match for the orbital defence that had been created. 

## Intergalactic Ambassadors

When diplomacy extended beyond smaller scales within the governments of planets, it became a challenge to adjust the same models
to the realities of intergalatic travel. The diplomat Andal Andaliman pioneered a technology that, using a combination of 
long-distance communication, automatic translation, and molecular reconstruction, would allow for the establishment of Embassies much like those once used only within planets.

## Galactic Harmony

With the many diverse species among the galaxy, the idea of joining peoples between different species under one banner has been 
extremely unpopular. However, it is not unheard of. With increasing developments in neuropsychology, it has become possible to 
make large groups of people agree to become citizens of an Empire led by a different species. The ethics of this new technology
have been intensely debated, though its many dissenters have all gradually come to side by it after mysterious conversations with
its proponents.

## Genetic Programming

It became a project of many Astrobiologists. It had long been possible to modify the genes of lifeforms to create variants, but
many wondered if it would be possible to make something entirely new. After much work an collaboration, the scientist Alexandra of Bronze 
successfully created a non-sentient creature much in her own likeness. Astroecologists quickly disovered the practical
applications, as it allowed them to create artifically stable ecosystems in many different environments by introducing appropriate
organisms.

## Mass Cloning

Cloning technology has long been known, but its applications have been limited due to their extreme cost and slow speed. But 
after long experimentation, the famed Doctor Reyna Kamion has perfected the method. Her technology has led to the establishment
of entire cities of people created purely from organic material. 

## Nanomarketing

The technology of Nanomarketing builds on the earlier concept of Hypercommerce, but extended to new heights. Nanomarketing allowed 
for the transmission of physical goods along long-distance communication lines. Although slower than Hypercommerce, the technology allowed ships to 
conduct business from a great distance, or even for factories to deliver their goods directly to consumers, without the need for ships to carry out the work.

## Quantum Computer

The famed researcher Anastasia Themils finally developed what had long been believed to be possible, but never able to be achieved. The technology allowed 
for computers to gain something like imagination and create extremely accurate extrapolations, even from seemingly unrelated data. 
The technology proved particularly revolutionary in the field of science.
